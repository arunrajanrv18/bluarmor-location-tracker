Moto g67 is the standout — stays under 6.5 m across all intervals. OnePlus A6000 degrades severely at 1 min (200 m). Samsung SM-M356B excels at 1s (3.08 m) but drifts at longer intervals.

At 1 min, OnePlus A6000, Xiaomi, and Moto g(60) all hit 400 m p95 — completely unusable for tracking. Moto g67 stays at 16.5 m even at 1 min.

Battery Drain

Percentage drop per hour — lower is better

Battery drain (%/h)
How much battery the location tracker consumes per hour at each update interval
OnePlus A6000 at 5s is a critical outlier at 16.76 %/h — its 3300 mAh battery is drained in ~6 hours. Moto g67 is the most efficient (0.46–1.88 %/h range). Intervals ≥ 15s keep all devices under 4.5 %/h.

Mean Temperature

Average device temperature during each test session (°C) — contextual, watch for thermal throttling above 40°C

Mean temperature (°C)
Higher update frequencies drive up CPU/GPS usage and device temperature
All devices stay in a safe thermal range. The 15s interval is notably the coolest (25–29°C mean) — likely GPS duty-cycle optimisation kicking in. At 1s, mean temps reach 34–37°C across all devices — sustained operation could trigger throttling on weaker SoCs.
